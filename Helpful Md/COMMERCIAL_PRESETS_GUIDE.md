# Commercial Presets Guide

## Purpose

This guide explains the `Commercial Preset` control in `Proposal Pricing`.

It exists to make repeatable quoting faster without creating a second pricing engine.

The preset layer only changes:
- pricing posture
- lifecycle allowances
- optional finance sensitivity
- proposal terms
- included scope / exclusions / next steps

It does **not** change:
- appliance list
- business classification
- phase topology
- inverter, battery, PV, MPPT, cable, or protection sizing
- engineering blocks or validation outcomes

## How To Use It

1. Pick the closest commercial posture in `Commercial Preset`.
2. Read the preview.
3. Click `Apply Preset`.
4. Continue editing the fields manually for the real site.

The preset is a starting posture, not a lock.

If you later tune the quote manually, that is expected. The tagged preset simply shows what commercial posture the quote started from.

## Current Presets

### Balanced Standard Quote

Use when:
- the job is a normal captive commercial quote
- you want a balanced default
- you do not need a special continuity or finance posture yet

What it emphasizes:
- standard pricing basis
- blended site-energy value story
- normal deposit, validity, and install-window assumptions

### Value-Led CapEx Quote

Use when:
- the client is price-sensitive
- the first goal is a lean but honest entry quote
- optional upgrades may be phased later

What it emphasizes:
- value package posture
- tighter commercial allowances
- stricter exclusions so the lean number stays honest

### Process Continuity Hybrid

Use when:
- the business cares about uptime
- the site may still depend on generator or hybrid assist
- the commercial story must stay honest about protected process loads

Good fit examples:
- bakery
- filling station
- cold room
- workshop
- mini-factory

What it emphasizes:
- premium allowance posture
- generator-energy value basis
- stronger lifecycle reserves
- clearer protected-load and assisted-load wording

### Premium Certified Quote

Use when:
- the buyer is quality-led
- branded equipment and documentation matter
- a more polished proposal posture is appropriate

What it emphasizes:
- premium pricing basis
- heavier documentation and handover wording
- stronger lifecycle reserve story

### Financed Growth Plan

Use when:
- the client wants to compare cash purchase versus financed acquisition
- preserving cash is part of the conversation
- you want a finance sensitivity starting point before a real lender path is confirmed

What it emphasizes:
- balanced technical package with finance-sensitive commercial framing
- debt-share, APR, term, and residual-value placeholders

Important:
This is advisory only. Replace the finance assumptions with the client’s real numbers before treating it as a serious finance discussion.

## What The Preview Means

The preview shows:
- the preset label and badge
- its summary and focus
- pricing basis and value basis
- deposit, validity, and install-window posture
- lifecycle posture
- finance sensitivity posture
- how many scope, exclusion, and next-step lines it will rewrite

If the preview says `Preview only`, the fields have not changed yet.

If it says `Applied to the current proposal`, that preset has already been pushed into the live commercial fields.

## Best Practice

- Use presets to start faster, not to skip judgment.
- Match the preset to the real client conversation.
- Re-check exclusions after every preset change.
- Keep finance assumptions conservative unless you have real numbers.
- Do not let a premium or continuity preset imply engineering headroom that the actual design does not have.

## Recommended Installer Habit

Use presets in this order:

1. Choose the closest preset.
2. Apply it.
3. Tune supplier pack and overrides.
4. Tune labor, soft cost, and margin.
5. Re-check `Commercial Strategy Recommendation`, `Supported Load Story`, and `Proposal Readiness`.

That keeps the quote posture fast and consistent without compromising the engineering truth.
