# Formal Study Scope Required Guide

## Purpose

This guide explains the `Formal study scope required` block now shown beside the heavier plant and utility-lane outputs.

It exists for one reason:

- to make the external engineering boundary explicit once the job has moved beyond bounded in-app study screening

## What This Block Means

The app can now carry a strong bounded study handoff:

- feeder and board rows
- breaker and cable carry-through
- PV, battery, AC, relay, transfer, and generator screening
- packet, case, and witness discipline

But that still does not mean the app has already completed:

- feeder fault / SCC calculations
- protection selectivity / coordination calculations
- formal interconnection or export-proof studies
- final commissioning / acceptance evidence packs

The `Formal study scope required` block closes that honesty gap.

## What It Can Show

Depending on the live scope, it can call out:

- `Feeder fault / SCC study`
- `Protection selectivity / coordination study`
- `Interconnection / export proof pack`
- `Commissioning / acceptance evidence`
- `Engineer-of-record handoff`

## How To Use It

Use it when the project has already crossed into:

- `Plant Engineering Surface`
- `Utility / Mini-Grid Engineering Surface`
- external relay / protection review
- external study pack
- authority-facing interconnection or witness scope

Treat it as the explicit next-step boundary:

- the app result remains the bounded basis and handoff record
- the listed items must leave the app for external engineering or authority workflow

## Where It Appears

The same block now carries through:

- the live plant result
- the plant feeder brief
- the feeder / protection study input sheet
- the study CSV data sheet as `formal_scope` rows
- the dedicated `Formal study work pack` export

## Honest Boundary

This block improves truthfulness and handoff clarity.

It does not claim that the app has become a full external study engine.

If you want the score to move beyond the current honest ceiling, the next phase is a genuinely separate study-calculation surface, not more wording or export polish.
